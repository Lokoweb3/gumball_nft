// HOW IT WORKS

PROVABLY FAIR MINTING

Our commit-reveal scheme ensures nobody can predict or manipulate your gumball's traits — not even us.

1

ORACLE COMMITS

The oracle generates a random secret and publishes sha256(secret + oracle_pubkey) on-chain. This happens BEFORE you pay — the oracle is locked in.

2

YOU MINT

You pay and provide your own random seed (user_seed). The oracle doesn't know your seed, and you don't know the oracle's secret.

3

ORACLE REVEALS

The oracle reveals its secret. The contract verifies it matches the commitment, then derives your traits from sha256(secret + slot_hash + user_seed + mint_index).

4

VERIFY ANYTIME

The commitment hash and user seed are stored on your gumball forever. Anyone can independently verify the randomness was fair using our verification tool.

// FAQ

FREQUENTLY ASKED

What blockchain is this on? +
Gumball Machine NFT is built on X1, a Solana-compatible blockchain. You need XNT tokens and an X1-compatible wallet (X1 Wallet or Phantom) to mint.
What does "100% on-chain" mean? +
The SVG artwork for every gumball is stored directly on the X1 blockchain — not on IPFS, AWS, or any external server. Your NFT can never be taken down or lost because the server went offline.
How do I verify my gumball is fair? +
Visit the Verify page and enter your gumball's serial number. The commitment hash and user seed are stored on-chain. You can independently recompute the traits to prove no manipulation occurred.
What happens if the oracle goes down? +
If the oracle doesn't reveal within 5 minutes, you can claim a full refund of your mint payment. The oracle auto-restarts via PM2 and the team is alerted via Telegram monitoring.
Can I sell my gumball? +
Yes! The built-in marketplace lets you list gumballs for sale at any price, make offers on others' gumballs, and trade directly. A 5% royalty goes to the treasury on every sale.
How does burn-to-upgrade work? +
Burn multiple gumballs of the same rarity to receive one gumball of the next rarity tier — guaranteed. For example, burn 5 Commons to get 1 Uncommon. Each upgrade also costs the current mint price as a fee.
Is the smart contract audited? +
The contract has undergone 5 rounds of security audit with 17 findings identified and resolved. The code is open source on GitHub for anyone to review.
